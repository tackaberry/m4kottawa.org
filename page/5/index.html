Mustaches for Kids is Back

M4K is back and better than ever.

We are happy to announce the FIFTH ANNUAL MUSTACHES FOR KIDS MUSTACHE GROWING CONTEST AND FUNDRAISER. This letter is your official personal invitation to greatness.

Last year, you, our devoted mustachees, raised $20,000 for Make a Wish Eastern Ontario. For more details on how your efforts helped, I have posted a letter from the people at Make a Wish: http://www.m4kottawa.org/2009/10/06/how-our-stachely-efforts-in-2008-helped-others/

So sharpen up your razors because Clean Shave Day is October 30, only a couple short weeks away.  We will be meeting at the Clocktower on Bank St to celebrate this loss of innocence. If we’re going to continue to beat our fundraising record, we’re going to have to bring our A-game this year. Encourage your friends/co-workers/family to join as well. “A single hair is nothing on its own, but united they form a mighty Mustache!”

Ready, Set, Stache Bash

Hello stachers,

We are in the home stretch – 2 days to Stache Bash. Reminder, and error correction from last week, please arrive at 7:30pm at Zaphods. We will be getting underway promptly. The address is: 27 York Street in the Market. Dress to impress or dress to kill – which ever suits your fancy.

Reel in those bucks over the next couple days and bring it in with you on Friday. If you have a chance, please have your cash changed into a cheque – your cooperation is really appreciated. All cheques must be made out to Make-a-Wish.

If you can’t make it on Friday let us know. We will find a way to collect your pledges and get you a t-shirt.

A big thanks to Jose at Joe Mamma’s. We will have some gifts from Joe Mamma’s to auction off. So, if you’re a boarder or skiier or like to look like one, you’ll dig this stuff.

If you have any questions, leave a comment on the blog and we’ll get it answered.

Enjoy your last week of sweet stacheyness.

Brett Tackaberry

Sens look to mustaches for help

It’s not only the kids that are hoping to benefit from a bunch of men growing mustaches in a somewhat organized effort.  The Ottawa Senators, in the midst of a winless slump, have given up on practicing their hockey skills like shooting and passing and are instead looking to the magic powers of mustaches to help them find their winning ways.

We can only expect that M4K Ottawa will benefit indirectly from this valiant hair-growing effort.

November 22nd, 2008 by Steve Palmer

Carpe Mustache

We have just passed the mid-point in our campaign and things are going very well. Some time over the last week, you would have slipped passed the “you don’t usually see younger fellows wearing a mustache” point in your growing that simply confirms your impending stachehood. Good growing.

This week we will again meet at the Clocktower at 8pm. Look for us upstairs as there is another function downstairs. Pictures will be taken, beers will be drank, and the regular tomstachery will ensue.

But how goes the pledging? Great, to hear it. If you’re anything like me you probably sent out an email to friends, family, colleagues, clients, enemies, ex-girlfriends, etc. You may get the occasional response like “I made a donation through Pay Pal. It is more of a sympathy donation for the difficulties you have in growing a moustache rather than for your success. Nevertheless, the cause is worthwhile and I should not penalize the charity because of your problems. Cheers!”. But you wouldn’t be doing this if you can’t have a chuckle at yourself.

Stache bash — t minus 15 days and counting.

That’s all folks. Over and out. Good hunting. See you on Friday.

Brett Tackaberry
